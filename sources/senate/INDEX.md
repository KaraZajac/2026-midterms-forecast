## Senate Control Math

The current Senate balance (as of May 25, 2026, post-Mullin resignation + Armstrong appointment) is:

- **Republicans: 53** (50 elected + Husted-OH appt + Moody-FL appt + Armstrong-OK appt; note Vance was elected R-OH but resigned; Rubio was elected R-FL but resigned; Mullin was elected R-OK but resigned — all replaced by R appointees)
- **Democrats: 45**
- **Independents caucusing with Democrats: 2** (Angus King-ME, Bernie Sanders-VT)
- **Effective: 53 R / 47 D-caucus**

### Number Each Party Needs to NET in 2026

- **To take majority (51 seats),** Democrats need to net **+4 seats**. (Achieves 51-49 if they net exactly 4; a 50-50 Senate with a Republican VP keeps R control. Democrats must reach a clean 51.)
- **Republicans need to net 0** to hold control. With a GOP VP (Vance) breaking ties, an R majority of 50 — including the 53 they already have — is sufficient.

### Realistic Flip Scenarios

To net +4, Democrats almost certainly need to: hold Georgia (Ossoff), hold Michigan (open), AND flip 4 of {Maine, North Carolina, Ohio-Sp, Iowa, Alaska, Texas}. They will also have to defend New Hampshire and Minnesota — both Lean/Likely D but in environments where any wave shift could threaten them.

Practical math (May 2026 consensus ratings):
- **Likely D holds:** GA (Ossoff Lean D), NH (Lean D), MN (Likely D)
- **Realistic D pickups (Toss-up / Lean):** ME (TU), NC (Lean D), MI hold (TU, open D), OH-Sp (TU), and second-tier IA (Likely R), AK (Lean R), TX (Likely R)
- The cleanest +4 path: hold all D seats, flip ME + NC + OH-Sp + 1 of {IA, AK, FL-Sp, TX}.

If Democrats flip only ME + NC = +2 net, R retains 51-49. ME + NC + OH-Sp = +3 net, 50-50 with VP tiebreak = R majority. **Four flips required, three of them currently rated as competitive Republican-held; the fourth is the structural challenge.**

## Headline Battleground

**Genuine Toss-ups:** Maine, Michigan, Ohio (Special).
**Lean / Toss-up split:** Georgia, North Carolina.
**Lean D:** New Hampshire.
**Likely R with upside for D:** Iowa, Alaska, Texas.
**Likely D:** Minnesota.

Eight states will absorb the lion's share of outside money: AK, GA, IA, ME, MI, NH, NC, OH-Sp. That is the consensus battleground, and the seats that will decide Senate control.

# 2026 Senate Fundraising — Q1 2026 FEC + Outside Group Reservations

**As of: 2026-05-25**

Two stories dominate the Q1 2026 fundraising picture. First, Democrats outraised Republican incumbents and frontrunners in virtually every competitive race — Cooper outraised Whatley 2.8:1, Ossoff banked a $31.7M cash-on-hand war chest (the largest on the map), and Mallory McMorrow / Abdul El-Sayed each outraised the establishment-favored Haley Stevens in Michigan. Second, the **Senate Leadership Fund (SLF)** announced a record initial **$342M reservation** across eight battleground states, while **Senate Majority PAC (SMP)** has announced over $165M in matching reservations in the same states. The independent-money fight will be largest in **Ohio ($79M SLF, $46.4M SMP)** and **North Carolina ($71M SLF, $31.4M SMP)**.

## Key Observations

- **Map convergence:** SLF and SMP target the same eight states — AK, GA, IA, ME, MI, NH, NC, OH. That is the consensus battleground.
- **Notable absence:** Neither group has reserved ad time in Texas, Florida, Minnesota, Nebraska — all of which have polling signals suggesting at least marginal competitiveness.
- **Spending tier asymmetry:** SLF is outspending SMP roughly $342M to $185M in announced first-tranche reservations — about 1.85:1. This compensates for the Democratic candidate fundraising edge in most of the same states.
- **EMILY's List:** has endorsed Pappas (NH), Stevens (MI), Craig (MN), Vindman (FL), and is independently active in support of female Democratic candidates; their published reservations are smaller and not yet itemized state-by-state in May 2026.

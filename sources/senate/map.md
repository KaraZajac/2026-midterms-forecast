# 2026 Senate Map — Class 2 Seats

**As of: 2026-05-25**

The 2026 Senate cycle is Class 2: 33 regular elections, plus two specials (Florida and Ohio) triggered by Trump-administration appointments — Marco Rubio (FL) became Secretary of State, JD Vance (OH) became Vice President. A third effective open seat opened in **Oklahoma** when Sen. Markwayne Mullin (Class 2) resigned in March 2026 to become Secretary of Homeland Security; Gov. Stitt appointed oil & gas executive Alan Armstrong, who is not running for the full term. That gives **35 seats on the ballot in 2026**, 23 of them defended by Republicans and 12 by Democrats — a structurally GOP-favorable map even with the four-seat path Democrats need to flip control.

Eleven Class 2 incumbents are not seeking re-election: four Democrats (Durbin-IL, Peters-MI, Smith-MN, Shaheen-NH) and seven Republicans (Tuberville-AL [running for governor], Ernst-IA, McConnell-KY, Cassidy-LA [defeated in primary], Daines-MT, Tillis-NC, Lummis-WY). Bill Cassidy's primary loss in Louisiana is the first time an elected incumbent senator has lost renomination since Richard Lugar in 2012. The combined retirement and open-seat list creates a wide battleground but a narrow set of true tossups (Maine, Michigan, Ohio-Sp, with NC and GA on the edge).

## Open Seats Summary (decisive for control)

**Open Democratic-held (4 + 0 specials):** IL, MI, MN, NH
**Open Republican-held (8 + 2 specials + LA-primary loss):** AL, IA, KY, LA, MT, NC, WY, plus FL-Sp, OH-Sp, OK-Sp.

Of these, the truly competitive opens are MI (D), NH (D), IA (R), NC (R), OH-Sp (R), with LA, KY, MT, AL, FL-Sp, OK-Sp, WY all rated solid/likely R.

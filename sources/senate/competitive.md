# 2026 Senate — Competitive Race Deep Dives

**As of: 2026-05-25**

This file covers every race at Toss-up or Lean from any of the three major handicappers, plus the high-Likely races where outside money is being spent (Iowa, Texas). The genuine battleground universe is **Maine, Michigan, Ohio-Sp, North Carolina, Georgia, New Hampshire**, with **Iowa, Texas, Alaska, Minnesota, Florida-Sp** as second-tier watch races. Republicans must defend Maine, Ohio-Sp, NC, IA, TX, AK, FL-Sp; Democrats must defend GA, MI, NH, MN.

## Maine — Susan Collins (R-i) vs. Graham Platner (D, presumptive)

The only Republican seat in a Harris-won state. Collins, 5-term incumbent, faces former Marine and oyster farmer Graham Platner, who has consolidated the Democratic field after Gov. Janet Mills declined to run. David Costello (2024 nominee) is also in the primary. Ratings: **Cook Toss-up, Sabato Toss-up, Inside Elections Tilt R**.

- **Candidate quality flag (D):** Platner is an insurgent with no prior elected office but has out-polled Mills and Collins in head-to-head trials; consolidated establishment + grassroots support.
- **Candidate quality flag (R):** Collins is the strongest GOP brand in Maine, never lost a race, chairs Senate Approps. But job-approval has slipped under Trump 2.0 and PVI is now D+2.
- **Primary:** June 9, 2026 (Maine). Platner the clear favorite.

[Source: Pan Atlantic / Spectrum News, https://spectrumlocalnews.com/me/maine/politics/2026/05/20/platner-leads-collins-in-new-poll-of-maine-senate-race]

## Michigan — OPEN (Peters retiring)

Cook/Sabato/IE all **Toss-up**. Open Democratic seat in an R+0 (Cook PVI EVEN) state Trump won in 2024.

- **D field:** Rep. Haley Stevens, state Sen. Mallory McMorrow, physician Abdul El-Sayed (Sanders/AOC-aligned, 2018 gubernatorial). Primary Aug 4, 2026. Emerson early May: El-Sayed and McMorrow tied at 24%, Stevens 13%. McMorrow and El-Sayed outraised Stevens in Q1 2026 — signals real progressive energy.
- **R field:** Former Rep. Mike Rogers (2024 nominee, narrowly lost to Slotkin) is consolidated frontrunner.
- **Quality flags:** Stevens = Frontline House moderate, organized labor base. McMorrow = viral "hate has no home" speech, suburban appeal. El-Sayed = ideological risk in a Trump-Trump state. Rogers = quality candidate but a known loser from 2024.
- **General trial heats (May 2026, Glengariff):** Rogers 43.8 / Stevens 41.5; Rogers 42.8 / McMorrow 40.7; Rogers 44.7 / El-Sayed 39.8.

## North Carolina — OPEN (Tillis retiring)

Cook **Lean D**, Sabato **Toss-up**, IE **Toss-up**. Highest-quality Democratic recruit on the map: former Gov. Roy Cooper (six statewide wins, never lost). GOP: Michael Whatley, former RNC chair (Aug 2024-Aug 2025). Trump endorsed Whatley.

- **Polling:** Carolina Journal / Harper (May 10-11): Cooper 49.8, Whatley 38.7 (+11). Emerson (earlier): Cooper +6.
- **Quality flags:** Cooper = top-flight; Whatley = first-time candidate, 52.7% of voters either have no opinion (19.3%) or have never heard of him (33.4%).
- **Money:** Cooper raised $13.8M Q1 to Whatley's $5M; Cooper $18.5M CoH vs Whatley $2.5M.

## Ohio (Special) — Jon Husted (R-appt) vs. Sherrod Brown (D)

Cook **Toss-up**, Sabato **Lean R**, IE **Tilt R**. Brown — defeated by Moreno in 2024 — is back. Husted was Lt Gov, appointed by DeWine after Vance vacated for VP.

- **Polling:** BGSU Democracy poll May 2026: Brown 49, Husted 48; OFT/Hart: Brown +3; Emerson Aug 2025: Husted +6. Polling has tightened toward Brown through 2026.
- **Quality flags:** Brown is a 3-term proven statewide winner; lost narrowly in a presidential R+11 year. Husted is competent but uninspiring, no campaign track record at top of ticket.
- **Money:** SLF reserved $79M (biggest single-state buy); SMP $46.4M.

## Georgia — Jon Ossoff (D-i) vs. R-nominee TBD

Cook **Lean D**, Sabato **Lean D**, IE **Toss-up**. Ossoff defending in a state Trump won by 2.2 in 2024. GOP primary did not produce a 50% winner May 19 — **runoff June 16** between Rep. Mike Collins and former Tennessee football coach Derek Dooley. Buddy Carter eliminated.

- **Polling:** Emerson Feb 2026: Ossoff 48, M. Collins 43 (+5); Ossoff 49, Dooley 41 (+8). Late primary polling showed Ossoff at/near 50.

- **Money:** Ossoff $31.7M CoH; M. Collins $2.1M; SLF $44M reserved, SMP $20M.

## Iowa — OPEN (Ernst retiring)

All three: **Likely R**. Rep. Ashley Hinson (R) cleared the GOP field early. Dem primary June 2, 2026 between state Sen. Zach Wahls and state Rep. Josh Turek; Nathan Sage also in.

- **Polling:** Data for Progress (vs. Ernst, pre-retirement): Sage 47–Ernst 45; Norris -6; Wahls -10. Limited post-retirement public polling vs. Hinson.
- **Quality flags:** Hinson = TV-ready 3-term House Republican, $6.5M CoH after $2.36M Q1. Wahls = young state Senate leader, $1.05M CoH. Turek = Paralympian, $757K CoH.
- **Money:** SLF $29M, SMP $13.4M. Big buys suggest both parties expect this to climb into Lean R if not Toss-up.

## Alaska — Dan Sullivan (R-i) vs. Mary Peltola (D)

**Lean R** universal. Peltola — former at-large US House member, defeated by Begich in 2024 — entered Jan 2026. Alaska's top-4 RCV system makes this more competitive than a typical R+8 PVI state.

- **Polling:** Alaska Survey Research: Peltola +5 to +7. Sullivan's reelect-deserve number at record low 41%.
- **Quality flags:** Peltola = strongest D recruit possible in AK. Sullivan = solid but uncharismatic.

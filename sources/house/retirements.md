# 2026 House Retirements and Open Seats

## Summary

As of May 2026, **57 House members and 2 non-voting delegates** are not seeking re-election — a record pace. The split is **23 Democrats / 36 Republicans**. 29 (13 D / 16 R) are retiring outright; 16 are running for U.S. Senate (7 D / 9 R); 11 are running for governor (1 D / 10 R); 1 R is running for state AG (Chip Roy). Republicans are taking the larger share of retirements, which historically signals an unfavorable cycle for the incumbent party. The most consequential open seats for the House majority fight are: ME-2 (Golden), NE-2 (Bacon), CA-48 (Issa), MI-10 (James), AZ-1 (Schweikert), IA-2 (Hinson), MT-1 (Zinke), NV-2 (Amodei), and FL-16 (Buchanan). The TX delegation is hemorrhaging incumbents (Crockett, Veasey, Doggett, Luttrell, McCaul, Arrington, Roy, Nehls, Hunt) but most are safe-seat replacements; the structural problem is that mass exits make primary outcomes harder to predict. NPR's tracker notes "more than 10% of Congress" won't return — the highest pre-election rate in modern history.

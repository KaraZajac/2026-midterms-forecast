# 2026 House Forecast — Source Material Index

Raw inputs for the Monte Carlo 2026 U.S. House forecast. All files compiled May 25, 2026 (~5.5 months from Election Day, Nov 3 2026).

## Files

- **[ratings.md](./ratings.md)** — Cook, Sabato, Inside Elections district-by-district ratings for ~55 competitive seats. May 2026 updates.
- **[retirements.md](./retirements.md)** — All 57 House members + 2 delegates not seeking re-election. 23 D / 36 R. Includes Senate, gov, AG runs and pure retirements.
- **[redistricting.md](./redistricting.md)** — Mid-decade map changes for 2026: TX +5R, CA +5D, FL +4R, OH +2R, MO +1R, NC +1R, TN +1R, UT +1D. Net ~R +7 structural shift. VA, NY, MD, IN attempts failed.
- **[fundraising.md](./fundraising.md)** — Q1 2026 FEC filings. DCCC vs NRCC parity. HMP ($272M) vs CLF ($153M) ad reservations. Candidate-level Democratic dominance in competitive races.
- **[generic_ballot.md](./generic_ballot.md)** — May 2026 generic ballot averages: D +6.6 across major aggregators. NYT/Siena +11; AtlasIntel +14. Trajectory clearly Democratic since Feb.
- **[specials.md](./specials.md)** — 105+ specials since Nov 2024. 30 R→D flips, 0 R gains. Median D overperformance +10.4 vs 2024 presidential baseline. Includes federal specials (TX-18, GA-14, NJ-11, TN-7, VA-11) and state-leg aggregate.
- **[approval.md](./approval.md)** — Trump approval ~38-40% / disapproval ~56-58% / net ~-18 to -20. Quinnipiac May 14-18: 34/58. Lowest of either Trump term. Decline driven by tariff inflation.

## Top-Line Signals

| Indicator | Reading | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Generic ballot | D +6.6 | Wave territory; comparable to May 2018 (D +6.8 → D +40) |
| Trump approval | -19 net | Historically associated with 25-40 in-party seat losses |
| Special-election overperformance | D +10.4 median | Confirms wave environment |
| Retirements (D / R) | 23 / 36 | R-heavy = signal of unfavorable cycle for in-party |
| Redistricting net | ~R +7 structural | Partial GOP firewall against wave |
| Outside group ad bookings | HMP $272M vs CLF $153M | D heavily outpacing R reservations |
| Cook toss-ups | 10 D-held / 8 R-held | Surface symmetry, but R-held seats are most of the wave-exposure pool |

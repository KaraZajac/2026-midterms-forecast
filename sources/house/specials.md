# Special Elections Since Nov 2024 — Leading Indicators

## Summary

Special elections since November 2024 have been **overwhelmingly favorable to Democrats**. Across the 2025-2026 cycle there have been ~105 federal + state legislative special elections; Democrats have flipped 30 seats Republican-to-Democrat (12 in 2026 alone), Republicans zero in the opposite direction. **Median Democratic overperformance vs the district's 2024 presidential baseline is 10.4 points (MultiState Elections)** with mean ~9-11. The DLCC measures D +9 on average. NPR/Brookings analyses put the average lower at ~4.5-5.6 D overperformance using different baselines. This is comparable to early-2018 specials (which preceded a +40 D House gain) — early 2018 medians ran ~D +18 but moderated to ~D +2 by Oct 2018. Kyle Kondik (Sabato) finds specials since 1957 typically break against the in-party president, confirming the directional signal here. **Important caveat**: special-election overperformance has historically translated to ~half the magnitude in November regular elections (turnout composition differs).

## State Legislative Specials — Aggregate Findings

- **105+ specials in the 2025-2026 cycle**.
- **30 net D flips, 0 R flips** (12 in 2026 + 18 in 2025 incl. VA/NJ regular off-year gains).
- **Median D overperformance vs 2024 presidential baseline: +10.4 pts** (MultiState).
- **Mean D overperformance: +9-11 pts** (DLCC: +9; MultiState mean: +11.5).
- **Brookings average overperformance: +4.5 pts** (different methodology/baseline).
- **Ballotpedia: avg shift of +5.6 pts toward D in state-leg specials since Jan 2025**.

## Implications for Modeling

- Strong directional signal: a wave-like environment for Democrats, magnitude ~D +6 to +10 in turnout-weighted regular environment.
- Historic translation factor: special-election overperformance × ~0.4-0.6 ≈ November regular-election overperformance. So +10 in specials → +4-6 in November ≈ generic ballot of D +6.6 (consistent).
- The signal has been consistent and persistent across geographic regions and seat types — robust prior toward a Democratic House gain of 15-30 seats absent major intervening events.

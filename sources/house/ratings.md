# 2026 House Race Ratings — May 2026 Snapshot

## Summary

As of mid-May 2026 (~5.5 months from Election Day), the three major handicappers all show Democrats slightly favored to retake the House. Sabato's Crystal Ball (updated May 8, 2026) has 217 seats leaning D, 205 leaning R, 13 toss-up — Democrats on the cusp of 218. Cook Political Report (updated May 13, 2026) sees 18 toss-ups: 10 Democratic-held, 8 Republican-held. Inside Elections (updated May 21, 2026, per Wikipedia) tracks closely with Cook but rates a few seats one notch differently (e.g. WA-3 Lean D vs Cook Toss-up; NC-11 Toss-up vs Cook Likely R; OH-9 Tilt R vs Cook Toss-up). Recent movement has been almost entirely toward Democrats — Sabato shifted 8 R-held seats toward Democrats in the May update; Roll Call reported 9 moves toward Democrats from Inside Elections in March 2026.

Key anomalies / things to flag for the model:
- **OH-9 (Kaptur, D)**: Cook/Sabato Toss-up but Inside Elections has it Tilt R (flip). Marcy Kaptur is one of the longest-serving members but the district was redrawn redder. Could be mis-categorized as Toss-up if 2024 baseline understates GOP lean.
- **NY-17 (Lawler, R)**: Cook Toss-up but Sabato Lean R. Lawler has been a strong fundraiser; his retirement watch list status as a gubernatorial candidate has been resolved (he's running for re-election).
- **FL-9 / FL-14 (D-held)**: Both rated Likely R / Lean R flip — these are the result of Florida's May 4, 2026 mid-decade redraw which created 4 new R-leaning seats.
- **WA-3 (Gluesenkamp Perez, D)**: Cook Toss-up; Sabato Lean D; Inside Elections Tilt D. Three different ratings — high uncertainty.
- **MI-10 (open, R after John James left for governor)**: Cook Lean R, Sabato Toss-up — a likely model-disagreement zone.

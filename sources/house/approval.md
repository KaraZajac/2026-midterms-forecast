# Trump Approval — May 2026

## Summary

President Trump's approval rating in May 2026 averages **approximately 38–40% approve / 56–58% disapprove**, with a net of roughly **-18 to -20 points** — the lowest of either Trump term. The decline accelerated through Q1/Q2 2026 driven by tariff-driven inflation, "stagflation" concerns, and a decline in Republican approval on economic handling (-15 points GOP-only April→May). Quinnipiac's May 14–18 poll showed 34% approve / 58% disapprove, a cycle low. Aggregators vary by methodology: USPollingData and Silver Bulletin run ~38–39% approve; RealClearPolitics ~40%. Net approval at -18 to -20 is historically associated with significant in-party midterm losses (Biden 2022 was net -10 with R +9 House gain; Obama 2010 was net -5 with R +63; Trump 2018 was net -9 with D +40).

## Trajectory

- **Jan 2026 (inauguration anniversary)**: 45–47% approve
- **Feb 2026**: 43–45%
- **Mar 2026**: 42%
- **Apr 2026**: 40%
- **May 2026**: **38–39%** (current; cycle low)

Decline of roughly 8 points over 5 months. Driven by tariff implementation, economic dissatisfaction, and erosion among independents (Quinnipiac shows independent approval at 34%).

## Implications for Modeling

- Historical relationship: a president at -18 to -20 net approval at the midterm typically loses 25–40+ House seats for their party.
- 538/Silver Bulletin's "expected seat loss" given approval ≈ -10 net → R seat loss of ~20–25; further decline raises that to ~30+.
- Approval is a strong but laggy indicator — model should weight a 6-month moving average, not just current snapshot, to avoid late-cycle whipsaw.
- GOP-only economic approval collapse is a red flag — base motivation problem for Republicans (consistent with NRCC's "enthusiasm remains a problem" warning per The Federalist).

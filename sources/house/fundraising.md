# 2026 House Fundraising — Q1 2026 (filed April 15, 2026)

## Summary

**Q1 2026 produced record fundraising on both sides.** The NRCC outraised the DCCC for Q1 ($47.1M vs $45.3M) and has slightly more cash on hand ($78.2M vs $70M), but Democratic House candidates dominated at the individual race level — multiple Democratic challengers outraised Republican incumbents in Q1, often by 2-3×. This pattern (committee parity but candidate-level Democratic dominance) historically correlates with a wave cycle for the out-party. Outside-group reservations are also at record levels: **House Majority PAC (D) has booked $272M and Congressional Leadership Fund (R) has booked $153M** in initial ad reservations — combined ~$425M, with HMP's reservation being its largest-ever first-wave.

Republicans have outraised Democrats at the committee level in several recent months, but Democrats have closed the gap and outraised the NRCC in some 2025 monthly reports per Punchbowl.

## Implications for Modeling

- Cash-on-hand gap at this stage modestly correlates with vote share (~1-3 points), but is highly endogenous to candidate quality.
- DCCC vs NRCC parity is roughly neutral as a signal; outside-group reservations strongly favor Democrats (~$272M vs $153M ≈ 1.78× advantage), unusual at this point in cycle.
- Use individual cash-on-hand differential as a soft prior for ~10-15 races where it's a clear blowout (>$1M differential).

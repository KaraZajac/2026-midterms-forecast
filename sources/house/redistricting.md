# 2026 Mid-Decade Redistricting

## Summary

The 2025–26 cycle saw an unusually aggressive mid-decade redistricting arms race, triggered by President Trump's push for Republican-friendly maps and Democratic counter-maps. **Net effect on the 2026 baseline (compared to 2024 lines): Republicans gained roughly +13 seats through redistricting (TX +5, FL +4, OH +2, MO +1, NC +1, TN +1); Democrats gained +6 (CA +5 via Prop 50, UT +1 court-ordered).** Net structural shift: ~R +7 before any wave effects. Virginia's Democratic counter-redraw was struck down by the Virginia Supreme Court on May 8, 2026, eliminating a hoped-for D +4. New York's attempt was blocked by SCOTUS in March. Indiana and Maryland tried but failed in their legislatures. Most challenged maps have cleared courts; Missouri's faces a citizen-initiative referendum that may suspend the map.

## Implications for Modeling

- Many "incumbents" in CA, TX, FL, OH, MO, NC, TN, UT are running in districts with materially different partisan composition than 2024 — applying 2024 margins directly without redistricting adjustment will mis-rate at least 20 seats.
- New numbered seats (FL-22, FL-25, TX-35, and several CA seats) have no incumbent and limited polling history.
- Treat district presidential lean (Daily Kos / Dave's Redistricting projections) as more reliable than 2024 House margins where lines moved.

# Generic Congressional Ballot — May 2026

## Summary

The generic congressional ballot in May 2026 shows **Democrats ahead by 6–7 points** across major aggregators, the largest Democratic lead of the cycle. RealClearPolitics' average has D 48.9 / R 42.3 (D +6.6), the largest D lead in their cycle tracking. Silver Bulletin's average stands at D +6.6 as of May 25. Recent high-quality individual polls have shown larger D leads: NYT/Siena (May 18) D +11; AtlasIntel (early March) D +14. Kondik / Silver both note this is virtually identical to the May 2018 generic ballot (D +6.8), when Democrats went on to net +40 seats. Live-phone polls (Quinnipiac, Marist, Fox) tend to show larger D leads; online/IVR (Emerson, Harvard-Harris) show smaller. The trajectory has been Democratic-favorable since February 2026, moving from ~D +3 to ~D +7 in concert with declining Trump approval.
